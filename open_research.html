Open research work


I helped build RepNote, a tool that makes replications more visible and linked to original research.

I am member of the ReproducibiliTea steering committee, a grassroots journal club initiative that helps researchers create local open research journal clubs. My focus is on the post-publication review project, which promotes transparent and inclusive research practices.

I co-ran the King's College London ReproducibiliTea journal club as a PhD student. The club writes public reviews of preprints, supported by ASAPBio.

With the support of the Improving Research Community Builder Award, I co-run Interdisciplinary Open Research symposia for early career researchers across the arts, humanities, social sciences, and medical sciences. A summary of our first meeting can be found here.

I also teach postgraduate students and talk with school students about science.
